FutuLabs

Create a device-based service in a weekend.

FutuLabs is the Futurice skunkworks initiative where we hack away on the latest and greatest in gadgets and technology. Our library includes devices like the Oculus Rift, Microsoft Band and various 3D printers. We’re constantly on the lookout for upcoming tech and make sure we get our hands on it as soon as possible.

What FutuLabs is for:

Hacker Happiness
FutuLabs stems from our software craftsmen honing their craft by playing with the coolest new gear. It allows our hackers to maintain and evolve their skills and enthusiasm, which is vital for creative thinking and idea generation. We don’t limit ourselves to consumer devices, but actively seek out and try to gain access to R&D projects that may someday become products. 

Designer Delight
The future is now and we want to design services for it. FutuLabs enables our designers to create experimental services for everyday things of the future. Designers can be hackers too.

Client Creativity
We want to provide our clients with the opportunity to be creative and see their business in new ways. Providing them with the latest upcoming technologies creates real life use cases for cutting edge technology.

How to win this track:
Show us a prototype of a service with great impact and a good story.

Know about some interesting gadgets that you’d want to hack on in this track? Tweet #futulabs @futurice and we’ll make sure to check it out.

SIMPLIFYING BUYING. AGAIN.

Buying things you love should be as easy as finding them. And paying shouldn’t be an issue in the buying process. With the featured Klarna API you can offer a pretty close to frictionless way for people to make purchases.

The solution is great for buying digital content of all kinds, paying for parking, pre-ordering take-away coffee or making micro donations to charities. We probably even missed a few use cases that would be just as great.

How to win: Use the API in the most creative way, from a UX, m-commerce, design or business perspective. Make a new kind of e-store, a brilliant marketing scheme or something we didn’t even think of. As long as the smooth and simple shopping experience is in the heart of your solution, it’s really up to you.

Improving Cities

Create an App using the Uber API

One of our core principals at Uber is to improve cities, which is why we are looking for developers too do just that. With the Uber API, developers have the ability to build applications that incorporate the entire Uber experience. In this track developers are challenged to use the Uber API to build an app to "improve the city". Developers may use any other APIs in the mix, but must include the Uber API as the primary use case.

How to win this track:

Use the Uber API to build the best app with the most impact. Our team focuses on the following criteria:

  1. Design
  2. Effective use of the API
  3. Originality
  4. Impact and utility

Teaching women to code

Technology should be accessible for everyone. Instead of just consuming and curating, we want to encourage and empower women to create things in the Internet. During a two-day workshop we coach women with no prior experience in programming to gain basic understanding on how the Internet works and what tools are used to build things online.

Rails Girls is a global movement that started in Helsinki 2010. In over 300 events in over 200 cities all around the world from Tokyo to Buenos Aires we have helped around 10 000 women take their first steps in programming.

Mehackit introduces youth to the maker side of tech via courses and events with robotics and programming. The aim is to give a bunch of empowering confidence, and to get a better understanding of how this world works and what epic stuff there is to build.
