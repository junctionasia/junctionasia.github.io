About

Hackathons are all about taking something you love, combining it with tech and learning something in the process. Junction brings together hackers, designers, the best partners and hardware for one weekend of hacking. Charge your laptops and bring your friends for an amazing event. Let's hack!

Pre-emptive Healthcare and Advanced Diagnostics

For whom?

If welcomes all developers, physicians and digital health enthusiasts to co-create both:
- pre-emptive healthcare services to support healthier life and/or
- advanced diagnostics services supporting a smooth recovery when something happens
If is the leading Nordic insurance company and committed to building a safer and healthier society. In order reach the goal, If actively seeks partnerships and collaboration with the start-up community.

Track content

During the event, participants will be creating new innovative health care products and services using relevant data, the leading API’s and devices, e.g. Apple Watch. The track is powered by IBM Bluemix. Bluemix is the leading platform-as-a-service solution and brings connectivity to IBM’s Watson, which in turn has, among others, multiple health related applications.

The Future of Commerce. Built by You.

Beacons, Apple Pay, Pinterest Buy - the world of commerce is changing. Both online and off, the technologies that are available to SMBs are bringing the power that was once only available to large retailers, to everyone. Join us at Junction and help bring over 165,000 of our merchants into that future.

Shopify is a leading cloud-based commerce platform designed for small and medium-sized businesses. Merchants can use the software to design, set up and manage their stores across multiple sales channels, including web, mobile, social media, brick-and-mortar locations, and pop-up shops. Shopify currently powers over 165,000 businesses in approximately 150 countries, including: Tesla Motors, Budweiser, Google, Wikipedia, LA Lakers, GoldieBlox, and many more.

Come imagine, develop and build the future of commerce with us.

Teaching women to code

Technology should be accessible for everyone. Instead of just consuming and curating, we want to encourage and empower women to create things in the Internet. During a two-day workshop we coach women with no prior experience in programming to gain basic understanding on how the Internet works and what tools are used to build things online.

Rails Girls is a global movement that started in Helsinki 2010. In over 300 events in over 200 cities all around the world from Tokyo to Buenos Aires we have helped around 10 000 women take their first steps in programming.

Mehackit introduces youth to the maker side of tech via courses and events with robotics and programming. The aim is to give a bunch of empowering confidence, and to get a better understanding of how this world works and what epic stuff there is to build.
